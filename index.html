ABOUT

Khalia is a soulful music artist of Hmong ethnicity whose sound blends heartfelt melodies with raw, resonant storytelling. Rooted in her heritage and shaped by her journey through an array of genres such as pop, R&B, country, and traditional Hmong music, her artistry explores themes of identity, resilience, and self-discovery, bridging past and present in a style that is both timeless and innovative.


From underground performances and competitions to hosting her first concert in 2024, Khalia has consistently captivated audiences with her powerful voice and authentic presence. Whether on stage or in the studio, she creates music that connects deeply, celebrating personal truth while resonating with universal emotions.


I grew up with very little, but music was the one place where I felt limitless. From as early as I can remember, I sang in my home, first inspired by traditional Hmong songs, the language of my childhood and the foundation of my voice. As I grew, I discovered an array of genres such as country, pop, and R&B, each one adding new colors and textures to my sound and shaping the versatile artist I am today.


Without formal training, I taught myself to sing by listening closely to artists on YouTube and imitating their voices. When electricity was scarce, I would write down song lyrics in notebooks so I could continue practicing even in the quiet. Through these small, persistent acts, I learned riffs, runs, belting, and the many tools that have become the craft I carry with me today.



The first time I stepped onto a stage at my elementary school talent show, I felt an undeniable connection. The moment I sang that first note, I knew the stage was home. From that day on, I embraced every opportunity to perform, from school rallies and graduations to competitions and community events, each experience reaffirming that music was where I belonged.


Today, my music reflects both where I come from and where I am going. It is a journey of resilience, identity, and connection, evolving with every song I sing and every stage I step onto.
